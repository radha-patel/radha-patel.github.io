Cashew Jelly (A Mobile Autonomous Robot)

MIT MASLAB (Mobile Autonomous Systems Laboratory)

My team and I built a robot to compete in an annual bracket-style competition hosted by MIT MASLAB featuring two robots on adjacent playing fields that compete to locate, pick up, and drop balls on the opposing team's playing field. Below are some details of the robot we built.

Mechanics
  • Intake: We created a shaft with two spinning gears lined with rubber bands as our intake mechanism + a 12V 500 RPM motor. After being consumed by the intake, the ball goes up a slight ramp and back down into the archimedes screw.
  • Archimedes Screw: We built an Archimedes screw using wooden dowels, a spiraling wire, and a lot of hot glue + a 12V 200 RPM motor to lift the ball to the top of the robot (18 inches). We have another constraining dowel, which keeps the ball in place as the screw pushes it upwards. We specifically chose this design to minimize the horizontal space needed to elevate the ball.
  • Draw Bridge: We have a servo with a dowel attached to it keeping a door closed to contain the balls on the topmost level of the robot. At the end of the allotted gameplay time (approx 150 seconds) and upon sensing that the robot is directly adjacent to the opposing playing field, the door opens to release the balls over separating wall.
Software

Below is a high-level overview of our strategy. We used a WebCam and OpenCV to identify the different colored balls and avoid walls.

  • Look for green or red circles:
    • If spotted, rank the coordinates of the circles based on distance and point value (determined by color) and prioritize those with higher scores. Turn towards ball until it is sensored on camera and drive forwards/intake for 3 seconds.
    • If not spotted, rotate until a ball is seen.
  • Avoid walls:
    • (1) Look for blue and yellow contours (tape lining top of walls) to identify walls.
    • (2) Set breakpoints for the y-positions of the contours - if the corners of the detected contour were greater than the breakpoints we selected (a wall was successfully detected), the robot would back up, spin in a random direction, and then move forward. If not (contour is from something else in background), the robot would continue movement as normal.
  • Dropping balls on opposing playing field:
    • 30 seconds before the end of the game, stop locating balls and rotate until detecting yellow contour (indicating separating wall between playing fields).
    • Center the contour and drive forwards until touching it.
    • Turn the servo to drop the draw bridge so that balls can fall to other side.
